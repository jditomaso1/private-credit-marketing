Automated Covenant Monitoring:
From Manual Spreadsheets to Real-Time Compliance

Published • 10–14 min read • AI in Credit

The private credit market has surged past $5 trillion, yet most funds still track covenants the same way they did 15 years ago — manually. Excel trackers, quarterly updates, inconsistent packets, and PDF-driven workflows dominate the space. It’s slow, error-prone, and fundamentally incompatible with today’s velocity and complexity.

Automated covenant monitoring changes everything. Using AI extraction, structured data, and real-time ingestion, modern platforms catch problems early, eliminate manual labor, and give PMs live visibility into leverage, liquidity, coverage, and every key operational KPI.

1. The Old System: Covenant Tracking by Hand

The traditional covenant workflow is fundamentally broken. Here’s what it looks like inside most funds:

1. Borrowers send quarterly PDFs

Often delayed, incomplete, or messy.

2. Analysts manually extract numbers into Excel

  • revenue
  • EBITDA
  • adjustments
  • total debt
  • cash
  • liquidity
  • capex
  • leverage ratios
  • coverage ratios

3. Someone updates the covenant model

Tests include:

  • leverage
  • fixed charge coverage
  • minimum liquidity
  • springing covenants
  • equity cures
  • carveouts

4. PM receives a summary

Usually late, inconsistent, and missing key signals.

5. Breaches discovered after the fact

By the time leverage jumps or liquidity collapses, the risk has been sitting unnoticed.

The manual model introduces lag, blindness, and preventable operational risk.

2. What Is Automated Covenant Monitoring? (Simple Definition)

Automated covenant monitoring is a system where AI:

  1. reads financials, certificates, credit agreements, and reports
  2. extracts all relevant covenant data
  3. calculates leverage, coverage, liquidity, and other tests
  4. evaluates those tests against deal-specific terms
  5. alerts the team instantly when something is off
  6. runs continuously rather than quarterly

Instead of analysts doing repetitive extraction, the system handles the grind. The result: instant visibility and early detection.
