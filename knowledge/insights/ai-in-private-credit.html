AI and the Future of Private Credit

Published • 12–15 min read • AI in Credit

1. The Private Credit Market Is Too Big, Too Fragmented, and Too Manual for Human-Only Workflows

Private credit grew fast — but the infrastructure never caught up.

Most credit funds, regardless of size, still run core processes on:

  • spreadsheets
  • shared drives
  • scattered PDFs
  • Outlook folders
  • junior analysts manually extracting covenants
  • associates building surveillance reports by hand
  • portfolio managers juggling dozens of trackers

This system worked at $1 trillion. It does not work at $5 trillion.

The structural challenges AI solves immediately

  • Document overload — every deal has a massive document pack.
  • Underwriting bottlenecks — deals move faster than teams can analyze.
  • Monitoring gaps — issues appear only after covenant failures.
  • Fragmented data — scattered between CRM, Excel, PDFs, and emails.
  • Analyst burnout — repetitive, high-stakes work that never stops.

AI isn’t replacing analysts — it’s doing what burns them out so they can focus on structuring and judgment.

2. AI Is Reshaping Every Stage of the Private Credit Lifecycle

AI is impacting five major workflows:

  1. Origination
  2. Underwriting
  3. Legal & Documentation
  4. Portfolio Monitoring
  5. Risk & Optimization

Stage 1: AI in Origination — Sourcing Deals Before Competitors

AI predicts borrower behavior, refinancing windows, leverage pressure, and sponsor activity — giving lenders an informational edge months before teasers circulate.
