Why Private Credit Needs AI Now

By Joseph DiTomaso · August 28, 2025 · 4 min read

The private credit market has exploded over the last decade. Assets under management have crossed $2 trillion in the U.S. and are forecasted to reach $3–5 trillion globally in the next few years. Demand is strong, but the operating infrastructure at many credit firms hasn’t kept pace.

Spreads are tighter, portfolios are larger, and risk is more complex. Yet many teams still run core workflows with Excel, email, and legacy systems. The mismatch is widening every quarter. This is where AI isn’t just useful — it’s necessary.

The Pressure Points

1) Deal Volume — More opportunities, less time. A diligence cycle that used to take three weeks now takes three days. Manual reviews of credit agreements, CIMs, and lender presentations can’t keep up.

2) Portfolio Complexity — CLOs, BDCs, NAV facilities, and hybrid strategies push exposures across geographies, sectors, and structures. Monitoring these in real time with spreadsheets and PDFs is unsustainable.

3) Margin Pressure — Fee compression is real. Investors want lower fees, higher net returns, and better transparency. That leaves little room for bloated headcount or inefficient processes.

Where AI Actually Delivers

Sourcing & Screening: Summarize a 300-page credit agreement in minutes, extract terms, and flag covenants for human review.

Structuring: Compare live deals to historical comps to identify pricing and covenant gaps before negotiations.

Monitoring: Run continuous compliance checks and risk alerts, and produce dashboard-ready outputs without the spreadsheet grind.

Optimization: Model loan swaps or stress scenarios in minutes instead of days; keep portfolios inside mandate while preserving yield.

What Early Adopters Gain

Faster diligence cycles, larger portfolios with leaner teams, better LP reporting, and the ability to protect margins. The real advantage isn’t just cost — it’s speed, scale, and confidence that risk is monitored continuously, not quarterly.

Looking Ahead

We’re early in the adoption curve. In a decade, it’ll be unthinkable to run a platform without AI-driven parsing, monitoring, and compliance infrastructure. The firms that move now won’t just “keep up” — they’ll define the standards the market follows.
